The HUV doc proposes a vacuum-sealed maglev tube launching a slender biconic waverider at Mach 20–25, with a self-applied silane → SiO₂ heat shield triggered on atmospheric contact. Tested against the existing RK4 + US Std Atm 1976 integrator at 15° / 30° / 45° elevations. Headline verdict: the "novel hypersonic" framing does not close — Mach 25 = 8508 m/s is slower than Manna-H's current 10,822 m/s, and silane combustion adds energy to the boundary layer rather than removing it. But the high-BC slender body geometry (BC ≈ 17,700 kg/m² vs Manna-H's 2,260) is exactly the next-rev redesign direction CLAUDE.md §10 already calls for. Salvageable pieces below.

Verdict

✓ Salvageable

  • BCHigh-BC slender body geometry (~17,700 kg/m²) — exactly the redesign direction CLAUDE.md §10 already calls for to close the Manna-H trajectory gap.
  • TPSUHTC + C/C + aerogel layer stack — conventional, well-characterised; usable on any Manna variant.
  • TUBEVacuum-tube assist on the BGKPJR rail — deserves its own concept doc in the BGKPJR repo. StarTram-class, never built, but the physics is real.
  • SPINSpin-stabilisation during the rail run — cheap, applicable to all three Manna variants.

✗ Does not close

  • SiH₄Silane self-applied heat shield is wrong physics. SiH₄ + 2 O₂ → SiO₂ + 2 H₂O is exothermic (~45 MJ/kg fuel) — adds energy to the boundary layer, doesn't remove it.
  • DEPOSITSiO₂ adhesion timing fails: vehicle moves 8–85 m during a 1–10 ms reaction. The deposit forms as a trail behind the vehicle, not as a coating ON it.
  • WEIGHT"Zero added weight TPS" contradicted by the doc's own layer stack. SiO₂ + UHTC + C/C + aerogel is pre-installed — that's PICA-X with a flammable topcoat.
  • PLASMAPlasma actuators replacing all control surfaces — control authority off by ~10⁵ at Mach 25 dynamic pressure. And the doc itself mentions morphing surfaces deploy after exit.
  • v_exitMach 25 = 8508 m/s is slower than Manna-H's current 10,822 m/s. The "novel hypersonic" framing is a step backward in raw v_exit.

◇ Open questions

  • PLANE35° N → equatorial LEO problem applies identically to HUV. Same matched-inclination tug fix needed (per CLAUDE.md §7.2).
  • EXITVacuum-tube end-cap engineering — plasma window, fast iris, ablating diaphragm. Never demonstrated at this exit velocity, repeatedly.
  • HEATSutton-Graves peak HF assumes R_nose = 5 cm. HUV's blunt-ogive geometry has a larger effective nose radius — peak HF likely lower than computed, but the trend holds.
  • BIO50–200 G launch envelope excludes biological cargo entirely. HUV is Manna-H-class only; not a fourth-variant slot.
